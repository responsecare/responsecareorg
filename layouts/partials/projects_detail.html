The Rwamwanja Project

Response Care has partnered with the First Child Centre based in Rwamwanja Resettlement Refugee Camp in Southwest Uganda, about 300km from Kampala.

The camp hosts over 100,000 refugees from the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Rwanda and is close to Uganda’s borders with both countries. There are many children in the camp, and due to the wars that displaced them from their homes, many of the children are effectively orphans and vulnerable.

Community leaders within the camp formed the First Child Centre in early 2017 to safeguard the camp’s vulnerable children from starvation and exploitation

First Child Centre gathers children in the camp daily, providing basic education and a safe space for them to gather and play. Response Care is currently evaluating how best to facilitate the educators and children with teaching and artistic resources in the camp.
